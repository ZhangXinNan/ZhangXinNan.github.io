Characters of the Game
Click any illustration to view full size. All characters drawn from the Te Rimu story and Tahia te Marae curriculum.
Te Rimu — Log Form
Taniwha · Disguised
"A gigantic, grey, knotty, knobbly old log" — with a golden ancient eye that winks at Aroha from the river.
Te Rimu — Eel Form
Taniwha · Frail · Ruha
"A GINORMOUS, slippery, slithery grandpa eel" — exhausted (ruha) in the polluted swamps of Oamokura.
Te Rimu — Restored
Taniwha · Kaitiaki · Guardian
Te Rimu fully revealed — majestic, glowing, restored. He recites the Pepeha as the river runs crystal clear.
Aroha
The Player · Protagonist
A curious young uri of Ngāti Ira. The first to see Te Rimu — "Are my eyes deceiving me?!" She starts the journey.
Tawa
Heroine · Community Leader
Neon jacket, arms raised, rallying the whānau. "The love for the land flowing strong through her veins."
Kaumātua
Elder · Narrator · Guide
The keeper of stories. Holds the walking staff and the pounamu. Narrates the whakapapa and guides the child through each zone.
🗺️ Places of the Rohe — Map Locations
Ko Matiti te Maunga
Matiti is our mountain
The mountain watches over the entire rohe. Zone 5 — Kupu Quiz. The ancient forest on its slopes is scarred by deforestation and must be restored.
Ko Waioweka te Awa
Waioweka is our river
The heart of the game — Te Rimu's home. Zone 1. The river runs dirty brown at the start and crystal clear teal when fully restored.
Ko Ōpeke te Marae
Ōpeke is our meeting place
The heart of the community. Zone 4 — the Big Cleanup begins here. Tawa leads from the ātea. Karakia starts the work.
Ko Mataatua me Tūwhenua ngā Waka
Mataatua and Tūwhenua are our canoes
The ancestral waka appear as a historical vision in Zone 2 (Hinerae). Tamatea paddles the Waioweka in his waka Tūwhenua.
